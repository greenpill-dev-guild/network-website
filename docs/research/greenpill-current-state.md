# Greenpill Network Current State

Research snapshot assembled on March 9, 2026.

## Executive read

Greenpill is no longer best described as a books-and-podcast brand with a chapter map.

The stronger public signal in 2025-2026 is:

- a decentralized network of chapters, guilds, and stewards
- a builder layer producing coordination and impact tooling
- a storytelling layer made of the podcast, Paragraph, newsletters, and working groups
- a coordination layer that increasingly overlaps with Regen Coordination
- a network trying to connect local regenerative work with onchain infrastructure

The website in this repo does not tell that story yet.

## What Greenpill publicly looks like now

### 1. Network, not just media

Greenpill publicly describes itself as a decentralized community made up of regional chapters and expertise-specific guilds.

That framing shows up across:

- network community calls
- chapter onboarding proposals
- the Hub / Regen Coordination forum
- Dev Guild events and repos

This matters because the site currently still centers `Learn`, `Participate`, and `Explore` around books, podcast, and generic links, instead of showing the network as a living operating system.

### 2. Chapters are still core, but more networked and region-aware

Confirmed public signals point to active or recently active chapter work across places including:

- Brasil
- Ottawa
- Toronto
- India
- Nigeria
- Côte d'Ivoire / CIV
- Koh Phangan
- Kenya
- China / Uncommons
- New York City
- Germany
- Cape Town
- London, Ontario
- Dominican Republic

The stronger 2025 signal is not just `many pins on a map`, but `regional coordination`.

One network strategy thread groups chapters and hubs into regional clusters such as:

- Great Lakes
- Sierra Nevadas & Rocky Mountains
- West to East Africa
- East Asia
- Brasil

That is a better website story than a static map alone because it explains how Greenpill scales.

### 3. Guilds, pods, and support roles are part of the real structure

Greenpill's public materials point to a network structure built from:

- members
- stewards
- chapters
- guilds
- pods
- network support roles

The Pod proposal is especially important because it reframes Greenpill as a place where someone can belong even if they do not yet fit a chapter or guild.

Publicly mentioned pod topics include:

- DeSci
- DePin
- Education
- Environment

This is one of the cleanest bridges between your workshop material and the public network footprint.

### 4. The Dev Guild is one of the strongest current proof points

The Dev Guild has one of the clearest and most current public footprints:

- recurring Builder Space events
- a public GitHub org
- live repo activity in late 2025 and early 2026
- tooling framed around impact measurement, coordination, capital allocation, and reputation

Current or recent repos include:

- `green-goods`
- `greenwill`
- `impact-reef`
- `cookie-jar`
- `gardens`
- `greenpill-commons`
- `onboarding-app`
- `allo-yeeter`
- `greenpill-hypercerts`

That means the website should not only talk about learning and local chapters. It should also show what Greenpill is building.

### 5. Greenpill Garden is a major bridge between chapters, stewards, and tooling

The clearest late-2025 signal is `Greenpill Garden - Season One`.

That program explicitly ties together:

- network identity
- steward development
- chapter and initiative structure
- CIDS-aligned impact reporting
- web3 tooling implementation
- IRL launch and closing events
- website revamp and new content

It also links Greenpill to a concrete stack of tools and service providers including:

- GreenWill
- Green Goods
- Public Good Staking
- Gardens
- Flow State
- Impact frameworks
- Karma GAP

This is a much richer story than the current site's `beginner / intermediate / advanced` ladder.

### 6. The Writers Guild and media layer are active, but underexplained

The Writers Guild shows up publicly through:

- the Greenpill Paragraph publication
- community call agendas
- a tooling survey and working groups
- book and publishing work
- writing and reporting support for Greenpill Garden

The podcast also remains active, with public signals showing:

- ongoing episodes into 2026
- a long back catalog
- discussion of a `Podcast Decentralization Roadmap`

So media is still central, but it should be shown as one layer of the network, not the whole identity.

### 7. Greenpill is moving toward coordination infrastructure and sustainable revenue

A major 2025 strategy thread proposes two north stars:

- bringing Greenpill onchain
- establishing revenue streams

Follow-on discussion expands that into a broader role:

- connecting and coordinating forms of capital
- acting as a bridge between web3 and impact communities
- becoming a platform for shared infrastructure, services, curation, and collaboration

Some of this is still proposal-stage rather than fully landed. But it is still important, because it shows what story the website needs to make legible next.

## Confirmed public signals vs. strategy signals

### Confirmed public signals

- Greenpill has active chapter, guild, and community-call infrastructure.
- The Dev Guild is shipping tools and running builder programming.
- The Writers Guild exists as a real content and publishing function.
- The network uses Luma, Discord, Hub / Regen Coordination, Charmverse, Paragraph, and GitHub as core surfaces.
- Greenpill Garden is a real program with dates, roles, budget, phases, and website implications.

### Strategy / proposal signals

- formal branch architecture for chapters, guilds, community, and support
- stronger regional hub model
- onchain reputation and governance
- sustainable revenue models
- podcast decentralization
- Greenpill as a bridge organization between web3 and impact communities

These are not all fully settled, but they are shaping the network's direction enough that the website should reflect them.

## What the current site in this repo under-represents

The local site snapshot in this repo is a single-page experience centered on:

- hero slogan
- chapter map
- books
- podcast
- a generic participation ladder
- generic community links

Main gaps:

- no clear explanation of chapters, guilds, pods, stewards, and support roles
- no visible Dev Guild product portfolio
- no visible Writers Guild / publishing pipeline
- no real explanation of Greenpill Garden
- no regional coordination story
- no partner / funder / public-goods operator journey
- no strong `why Greenpill exists now` narrative for non-Web3 visitors
- no chapter stories or proof of local outcomes
- no connection between media, community, products, and impact

## Implications for the website refresh

The website needs to shift from `content shelf + link hub` to `network narrative + proof of activity + role-based onboarding`.

The most promising framing is:

Greenpill helps local communities, builders, and storytellers coordinate regenerative action with web3-native tools, shared learning, and global support.

If that framing holds, the site should make these relationships obvious:

- Chapters create local presence.
- Guilds and pods create skill and topic depth.
- Builders create coordination tooling.
- Writers and media create sensemaking and reach.
- Greenpill Garden and related programs connect stewards, tooling, and real-world outcomes.
- Regen Coordination and aligned partners expand the network beyond Greenpill alone.

## Suggested steward discussion prompts

- Is Greenpill primarily a network, a media brand, a builder ecosystem, or all three?
- Which chapter and guild stories are strong enough to lead with right now?
- Which products or protocols deserve homepage real estate?
- Which audiences matter most in the next six months?
- What should a non-Web3 visitor understand in the first 30 seconds?
- Which parts of the current network are stable enough to present as core structure, and which should remain experimental?
